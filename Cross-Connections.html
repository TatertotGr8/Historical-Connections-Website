Overarcing Themes 

Colonialism and Decolonization

All four events involve some aspect of colonialism and decolonization. The Mongol Invasion of Kievan Rus and Korea, and the Spanish colonization of Western Sahara all show how external powers attempted to dominate and control other regions. The decolonization movements that followed in these areas, as well as the collapse of the Soviet Union, reflect a larger global trend towards independence and self-determination. 

Geopolitical Tensions

The events in Russia & Ukraine and Korea are both examples of geopolitical tensions between major powers. In the case of Russia & Ukraine, there is an ongoing conflict between Russia and the West over influence in the region. In Korea, the tension is between North Korea and the United States. Both situations highlight the potential for major power conflicts and the importance of diplomacy in resolving disputes.

Human Rights Abuses

The events in Chile and Western Sahara both involve human rights abuses and violations. The dictatorship of Augusto Pinochet in Chile was marked by widespread political repression and torture, while the Western Sahara conflict has resulted in the displacement and mistreatment of Sahrawi refugees. Both situations highlight the importance of human rights protections and the need for accountability for those who commit abuses.

International Involvement

All four events involve some level of international involvement or influence. In the case of Russia & Ukraine and Western Sahara, there is direct involvement by the United Nations. In Korea, the United States has played a major role in the conflict. And in Chile, the international community has been involved in efforts to bring accountability for human rights abuses. These situations demonstrate the importance of international cooperation in resolving conflicts and promoting human righs.
