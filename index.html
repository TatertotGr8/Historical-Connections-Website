Historical Nexus

Pathways High Junior Defense 2023
Tate Goetsch   

This project will study the historical connections between four different regions that have experienced political turmoil, conflicts, and interference from foreign powers. All four projects involve the examination of historical events and their impact on the societies and politics of their respective regions. The projects share a common theme of conflict and the effects of foreign interference. The regions in question are Russia and Ukraine, Korean society, Chile's political situation, and the Western Sahara conflict. The projects on Russia-Ukraine and Western Sahara both involve the issue of territorial disputes and the involvement of outside powers, while the projects on Korea and Chile both focus on the impact of foreign occupation on their respective countries. The project's goal is to provide a comprehensive understanding of the origins and implications of significant events, including the Mongolian invasion, the Coup led by General Augusto Pinochet, and the Green March. Various primary and secondary sources were utilized, such as historical records, archives, personal accounts, books, academic articles, news reports, and documentaries. All four projects aim to provide a deeper understanding of the origins of present problems and conflicts in their regions and to contribute to efforts towards reconciliation and peace.
