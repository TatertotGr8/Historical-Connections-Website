Shadow of Brutality:
The Roots of Chile's Political Turmoil

Abtract 

This historical project seeks to investigate Chile's political turmoil and conflict following the election of socialist Salvador Allende as president in 1970, his subsequent overthrow by General Augusto Pinochet's coup in 1973, and the resulting violence and human rights abuses under Pinochet's regime. The project will center on pivotal events such as the National Stadium tragedy and General Pinochet's arrest and imprisonment. The project will draw on both primary and secondary sources, including government documents, news reports, personal testimonies, books, academic articles, and documentaries, to provide a comprehensive understanding of the historical context and significance of these events, as well as their long-term impact on Chilean society and politics. The research will also investigate the complexity of Chilean history throughout the 1970s, investigating the links between Allende and Pinochet, as well as their opposing political views. Despite their disagreements, the two leaders had served in the military together, with Pinochet serving as Allende's army chief. The study hopes to provide insight on the complex political and social factors that molded Chile throughout this turbulent time in its history by investigating these links. The research will also investigate how events from the 1970s continue to impact Chilean society and politics today.

Salvador Allende Wins Chile Presidential Election 

Salvador Allende won the 1970 Chilean presidential election as the candidate of the socialist coalition, making him the first Marxist to be elected as president of a Latin American country through democratic means. Allende's presidency was marked by efforts to nationalize key industries and implement socialist reforms, leading to opposition from right-wing groups and eventually a military coup in 1973.
The economic and political changes initiated by Allende's government, including nationalization of industries and land reforms, caused significant opposition from the political right and business community. The opposition was further fueled by allegations of corruption and mismanagement within the Allende government.
